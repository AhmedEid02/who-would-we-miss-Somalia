A climate shock map can show where hazards occurred.

But the real decision question is: **who would we miss?**

Using SIHBS 2022, I built a Bayesian shock-to-action targeting analysis to estimate which households were climate/livelihood shock-exposed, food-security stressed or using erosive coping, and unreached by formal support.

Key finding: formal support reached only 3.8% of households after shock, while 40.1% were classified as high-risk and unreached.

At 20% support coverage, Bayesian risk targeting reached 34.0% of high-risk unreached households, outperforming climate/livelihood shock-only targeting (27.8%) and poverty-only targeting (19.5%).

The goal is not to replace humanitarian judgment. It is to make early action more transparent, probability-based, and harder to ignore.

From risk mapping to action intelligence.
